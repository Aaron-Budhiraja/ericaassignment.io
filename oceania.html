New Zealand: Land of Natural Wonders

Things to See and Do

South Island Highlights

Milford Sound - The crown jewel of Fiordland National Park. Take an early morning cruise to see waterfalls, seals, and dolphins against the backdrop of Mitre Peak. Stay overnight in Te Anau for the best access. Pro tip: Book a scenic flight back to Queenstown for breathtaking aerial views of the Southern Alps.

Adventure Capital

Queenstown - The adventure capital of the world offers year-round thrills. Try bungy jumping at the original Kawarau Bridge site, ski at Coronet Peak in winter, or hike the Routeburn Track in summer. The region's wineries provide perfect relaxation after adrenaline activities.

Mount Cook National Park - Home to New Zealand's highest peak and some of the clearest skies in the world. The Hooker Valley Track offers accessible hiking with stunning glacier views. Stay at the Hermitage for world-class stargazing opportunities.
